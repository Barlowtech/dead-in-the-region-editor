THE CASE:
[Type: Real]

The legendary real case of Belle Gunness — a Norwegian immigrant who settled in La Porte Indiana in the early 1900s and became one of America's most prolific female serial killers. She placed personal ads in Norwegian-language newspapers across the Midwest luring suitors to her farm with promises of marriage. They arrived with their life savings. They were never seen again. At least 14 confirmed victims possibly more than 40. Her farm burned down in 1908 with a headless female body inside — and the question of whether Belle actually died has never been conclusively answered.

CASE HOOK (the single most compelling detail):
A woman killed possibly 40 people by placing DATING ADS in newspapers. She lured men to a farm with romance and murdered them for their money. And she may have faked her own death and gotten away with it.
